# ADUYes — Competitive Analysis (2026-06-14)

Grounded in live research of the ADU-tool landscape.

## Landscape

| Competitor | What it is | Strength | Limitation vs ADUYes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Symbium** (symbium.com) | Map/parcel-level ADU feasibility; city partnerships (e.g. Chula Vista); vendor marketplace | **Address-level zoning accuracy** — knows your specific lot's rules | **California + partner cities only**; no national coverage |
| **Villa** (villahomes.com) | Prefab/turnkey ADU builder with a cost estimator | End-to-end build service | CA-centric; estimator is **lead-gen for their own builds** (single-vendor) |
| **Maxable** (maxable.com) | ADU consulting marketplace + content | Hand-holding + vetted pros | CA-focused; consulting funnel, not a self-serve national tool |
| **Builder calculators** (True Built, Better Place, CCS, aduplanet) | Cost calculators on builder sites | Quick estimates | Biased to that builder; usually single-region; no rules/feasibility |
| **ADUscale "Reality Check"** | 2-min CA qualification quiz | Fast | CA only |

Cost consensus across sources: **$180k–$400k** depending on type/site work — this **validates ADUYes's cost model** (our detached range lands in this band after the regional index).

## ADUYes's positioning & advantages

1. **National coverage** — ADU rules + cost for **all 50 states + DC** and 240+ cities. Symbium/Villa/Maxable are essentially **California-only**. This is our defensible SEO + TAM advantage (programmatic long-tail nationwide).
2. **Vendor-neutral** — we don't sell one builder's units, so estimates aren't biased; lead-gen refers to *vetted* builders (better for the homeowner, higher trust).
3. **Transparency** — the `/methodology` page openly explains every assumption; competitors' calculators are black boxes.
4. **Decision depth, not just a number** — feasibility flags (size/setback/parking/owner-occupancy/lot coverage) **plus ROI/rental income** in one view. Most competitors give cost OR a yes/no, not both + investment math.

## Honest gaps (future roadmap, mostly external dependencies)

- **Parcel/address-level feasibility** (Symbium's moat) — requires per-jurisdiction zoning datasets / GIS APIs (expensive, jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction). ADUYes is state/city-level today; address-level is a future paid-data integration.
- **ADU visualization / siting on the lot** — Symbium renders allowed placement; out of scope for the free tier.
- **Real builder marketplace** — currently lead-gen via the report form (honest mailto); a true matched-marketplace needs builder onboarding + a datastore (see README "Lead capture").

## Takeaway

ADUYes wins on **breadth (national) + neutrality + transparency + investment insight**. We deliberately don't compete with Symbium on CA parcel-level depth; we own the **"ADU rules & cost for *any* US state/city"** query space, which the incumbents leave wide open.
